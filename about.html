Manifesto

OB1 stands for “Obekväma arbetstider 1” in Swedish — or “Inconvenient Working Hours – Category 1” in English.

The term was coined in Swedish labor law to describe evening, night, and weekend shifts — working hours often assigned to invisible, low-control jobs in restaurants, logistics, cleaning, and private security.

These sectors differ, but share one trait: they typically operate on thin margins, where labor costs are minimized and protections remain vague. We understand that.

The idea for OB1 emerged in April 2025, after recovering three months of withheld unemployment benefits — a turning point made possible by navigating administrative fog through assertive, strategic communication.

The underlying experience took place in winter 2024, while working as a dishwasher at Quan, a high-end restaurant in Mälmo. Despite a high workload and overall good feedback, no written contract was ever issued, and wages were paid late. The employer's classification of the departure led to a suspension of benefits....

OB1 is about knowing what you accept — and making sure others don’t go too far. It’s for workers who accept the reality of such jobs, but who want to set limits, to protect what little is owed to them — and to respond strategically when lines are crossed.

It is not about seeking representation or relying on distant institutions but for connecting with those who have faced informal contracts, delayed wages, intimidation, or unclear employment status — and those who’ve found ways to document, challenge, or navigate such situations.

We don’t rely on lawyers — most of us can’t afford them. We don’t turn to unions either — time is short, fatigue is real, and responses are often too slow. Instead, in 2025, we leverage permissionless tools, platforms, apps and tactics that don’t require approval to act.
