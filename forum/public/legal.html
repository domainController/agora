OB1 Circles Forum Manifesto

Legal Notice & Data Policy

Language and Accessibility

  1. This website is currently available in English for practical reasons. Many workers affected by unjust dismissals, particularly in the hospitality and cleaning sectors, do not speak Swedish fluently.
  2. A full bilingual version (Swedish/English) will be deployed shortly to ensure full accessibility for everyone concerned.

Purpose & Data Collection

  1. This site collects limited personal data solely to enable early access to the OB1 forum and to foster community support.
  2. Your submission allows us to contact you regarding shared legal concerns or workplace situations.
  3. We may notify you if other users report similar cases or mention the same employer.

Confidentiality & Consent

  1. Your data is never shared with third parties or employers without your explicit consent.
  2. The platform is not for denunciation but for empowerment, solidarity, and practical legal and administrative insight.
  3. You may request full deletion of your data at any time by contacting us at legal@ob1.se.

Forum and Commenting Infrastructure

  1. The OB1 platform includes both a forum (currently powered by Flarum) and a comment system (currently powered by Giscus, a lightweight tool built on GitHub Discussions).
  2. Giscus requires a GitHub account to participate in discussions. This choice was made for fast deployment and is not intended to restrict participation.
  3. Users who cannot or do not wish to use GitHub may contact us via email or participate through OB1 Circles.
  4. As the platform evolves, we may migrate comments or discussions to alternative tools (e.g., Commento or embedded forums), provided privacy and legal compliance are maintained.
  5. All significant changes will be clearly communicated via updated terms or page footers.

Origins & Purpose

  1. The OB1 initiative is rooted in lived experiences in hospitality and precarious labor ("OB" or "Obekväm arbetstid").
  2. Its goal is to connect affected individuals and discuss legal options and community strategies. Read the manifesto.

Legal Compliance

  1. All data is stored securely, complies with GDPR, and aligns with Swedish labor law.
